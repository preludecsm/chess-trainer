Personality Chess Trainer

3

Each opponent has a deliberate, exploitable bias — it will pursue its obsession even where that's a bad idea. Learning to spot and punish the pattern is the training.

Beginner
No bias — a clean, honest opponent at any depth.
SafeRandom
Never blunders outright, but has no plan at all. Practice building and converting small advantages.
WanderingQueen
Brings the queen out early and keeps her roaming. Attack her while developing — win the game on tempo.
PawnStorm
Hurls pawns at your king. Stay calm, defend precisely, and strike back in the center it left behind.
Fianchetto
Must have its bishop on the long diagonal and clings to it. Trade that bishop off, then own the weak squares.

How many half-moves ahead the bot calculates — its strength dial. Higher is stronger but slower to move: depth 2 is instant but weak, depth 3 is the sweet spot (a few seconds per move), and each step above that multiplies the wait.

Resign ends the current game early (Take Back can undo it, like any other move). Copy PGN copies the game — at any point, even after it's over — to your clipboard for pasting elsewhere. Analyze on Lichess opens whatever position is currently on the board in Lichess's own analysis tool.

Click any move to jump back and look at that position. From there you're not just looking — play a move and the game continues from that point instead, replacing whatever actually happened next. Only works on positions where it's your move; the status line says so if it isn't. Good for trying a different response to the same early decision as many times as you want.
